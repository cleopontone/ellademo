The problem

Hotel underwriting hasn't changed in decades.

Hospitality acquisitions still get underwritten the way they were twenty years ago — by hand, in a spreadsheet, against data that only describes the market you can already see. Five gaps keep showing up.

01
You're blind to the supply that hasn't opened yet.
The comp set only counts hotels already operating. The project approved down the street, the motels converting to housing, the national operator moving in — none of it shows up for years, yet it's exactly what you'll compete against at stabilization.
02
The renovation budget is a guess.
PIP cost lands in the model as a single per-key plug, untethered from what the work actually costs or who would do it. With pricing swinging from thousands to $40k+ a key, a rule-of-thumb number is how a deal quietly loses its basis.
03
One number stands in for the whole range of outcomes.
Most models output a single IRR from a single set of assumptions — false precision dressed up as confidence. Without probabilistic modeling, you can't say how likely you are to clear your hurdle or where the downside actually lives.
04
Your best analysts spend their hours typing.
Every T-12, rent roll, and assumption is keyed in by hand, cell by cell. The most expensive people in the room burn their time transcribing instead of judging the deal — and every manual hop is a place for an error to hide.
05
The operational upside is a black box.
The gap between as-is and stabilized is where the deal is won or lost, but it's sized by gut. Where the value really sits — normalized expenses, rate penetration, departmental margin — stays cloudy until long after the offer goes in.

The result: deals priced on partial information, slowly, with no read on the odds.

What ella does

ella closes all five gaps.

An AI underwriting platform that reimagines how hospitality deals get underwritten — from deal screen to stress-tested pro forma.

Fixes gap 01
Forward-looking supply scan
ella scans the submarket from public records — planning commission filings, town records, local press — and surfaces what's coming: new supply in the pipeline, supply coming offline, ownership and operator moves, and the regulatory climate. Every finding is cited and links to its source.
Fixes gap 02
PIP scope-to-cost estimator
Describe the renovation in plain language and ella decomposes it into a costed, scheduled bill of work: goods, freight, fuel surcharge, and labor, each with quantities, unit costs, and lead times. Costs anchored to real permitted renovations nearby, not a rule of thumb.
Fixes gap 03
Monte Carlo stress test
Probability of clearing the return hurdle, IRR percentiles (P10/P50/P90), DSCR-breach risk, and a tornado chart showing exactly which driver swings the result. Price the whole range of outcomes, not one number.
Fixes gap 04
Guided underwrite with sourced defaults
Driver by driver — revenue, expenses, PIP/capital, financing, exit — each with a defensible default and a one-line rationale. Editable and fast. Reads like a model an analyst trusts, not a form to fill out.
Fixes gap 05
A thought partner, not a form
On the judgment calls usually made in isolation — occupancy disruption during renovation, the ramp to stabilization — ella reasons through it with you. Deal-aware: it surfaces the supply entering in Year 2 that could cap your ADR growth.
